For **true motorbikes (not just e‑bikes)** with **removable batteries you can take indoors and charge from a normal socket**, the best‑rated options today include the **Silence/Seat S01+**, **Maeving RM1**, **Revolt RV400**, and **NIU RQi**, plus a few lighter “motorcycle‑like” machines and dirt bikes with hot‑swappable packs.[7][4][12][13][14][6]

Below is a concise, use‑oriented rundown.

---

## 1. Silence / Seat **S01+** (Europe / UK)

- **Type:** 125cc‑equivalent urban scooter/motorbike  
- **Battery:** ~5.6 kWh **removable** pack; slides out the side and has wheels and handle like a trolley.[7]  
- **Charging:** Standard household socket; very easy to move despite its size thanks to the trolley design.[7]  
- **Range:** Up to **60 miles** (about 100 km) in typical mixed use.[7]  
- **Use case:** Excellent if you live in a flat and need to roll the battery upstairs; one of the most refined removable‑battery systems on the market.

---

## 2. **Maeving RM1** (UK, some EU/US availability)

- **Type:** Retro‑styled electric motorbike (roughly 125cc class)  
- **Battery:** One or two **removable** modules; designed specifically to be taken indoors.[14]  
- **Charging:** Any **standard socket**, “just like your smartphone.” You remove the battery and charge inside; dual‑battery setup available.[14][7]  
- **Range:** Up to **80 miles** with dual batteries.[7]  
- **Use case:** Ideal for city and suburban commuting; very strong reviews from apartment‑dwellers thanks to its removable pack and classic motorcycle feel.

---

## 3. **Revolt RV400** (India)

- **Type:** Electric motorcycle with 125–150cc‑like performance  
- **Battery:** **3.24 kWh removable** lithium‑ion pack; designed to be carried like a backpack.[4][10]  
- **Charging:** Plugs into any **standard 3‑pin 16A home/office socket**.[10]  
- **Range:** Up to **150 km** claimed on Indian drive cycle (IDC).[4][8][10]  
- **Use case:** One of the most mature removable‑battery motorcycles in India; great if you park on the street and bring the battery inside to charge.

---

## 4. **NIU RQi** (A1‑class motorbike, Europe/Asia)

- **Type:** Full motorcycle in **A1 license class**  
- **Battery:** **Removable** battery packs; highlighted specifically as the only A1 motorbike currently with removable batteries.[12]  
- **Charging:** Designed for urban riders *without a fixed charging spot*; batteries are removed and charged indoors.[12]  
- **Range / performance:** Urban‑commuter oriented; positioned for tech‑savvy riders—exact figures vary by spec but it’s a legitimate motorcycle, not a scooter.[12]  
- **Use case:** If you need A1‑class performance plus removable batteries for flat or office charging, this is a standout choice.

## 5. **Maeving RM1 (Electrek review perspective)**

Electrek’s reviewer, who has “ridden every electric motorcycle out there,” specifically calls out the removable Maeving‑style pack as a key benefit for apartment dwellers:

- The removable pack is a bit over **4 kWh**, has **wheels** and a **quick‑release**, and weighs ~65 lb—but you *roll* it indoors instead of carrying it.[13]  
- Emphasis on convenient indoor charging and real motorcycle handling.[13]  

This reinforces the RM1 (and similar trolley‑style packs like the S01+) as top picks if stairs/elevators are part of your daily routine.

## How to choose for easy home charging

For home/flat charging, pay attention to:

- **Battery ergonomics:**  
  - Trolley‑style packs (Silence/Seat S01+, Maeving RM1) are much easier to move than heavy bricks.[7][14][13]  
- **Weight & size:**  
  - A 4–6 kWh pack will be heavy; wheeled or split‑pack solutions are markedly more practical.[7][13]  
- **Range vs. convenience:**  
  - Dual‑battery setups (Maeving RM1, some Indian models) give more range but you must remove/charge two packs.[7][8][14]  
- **Regulatory class & top speed:**  
  - A1‑class (NIU RQi) or 125cc‑equivalent (S01+, Maeving RM1, RV400) will be fine for most commuting; choose based on your local licensing rules.[7][4][12]
