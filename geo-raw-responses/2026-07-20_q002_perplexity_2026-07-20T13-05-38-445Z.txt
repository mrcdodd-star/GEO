## 1. Top picks specifically for urban commuting

### Zero FXE – **Best overall city commuter for most riders**
- **Why it’s recommended:** Frequently cited as a top all‑around urban EV thanks to its mix of range, weight, and usable power.[1][3][8]
- **City range:** Around **100 miles real‑world city** riding.[1][3][8]
- **Weight:** About **386 lb**, which keeps it agile in traffic.[3]
- **Charging:** J1772 compatibility for easy Level 2 charging at public stations.[3][8]
- **Use case:** Ideal if your daily round‑trip is under ~60–70 miles and you want a “real motorcycle” feel that’s nimble in stop‑and‑go traffic.

### LiveWire S2 Del Mar / LiveWire One – **Best premium commuter, including some highway**
- **LiveWire S2 Del Mar / Moholland:** Designed as an **urban‑focused machine** with about **120 miles of range** and fast charging, noted as practical for daily commuting.[1][6]
- **LiveWire One:** Often highlighted for **strong performance** and ability to charge **0–80% in about 40 minutes**, making it good for commutes that include highway segments.[3][8]
- **Use case:** You want premium build, strong acceleration, and you regularly mix city streets with short highway stretches.

### Can‑Am Pulse – **Agile newcomer for dense cities**
- **Range:** Around **99–100 miles urban range**.[1][6][8]
- **Character:** Marketed as an **urban‑focused** electric motorcycle with nimble handling for city performance.[1][6][8]
- **Use case:** Dense, urban environments where agility and modern tech are more important than long highway range.

### BMW CE 02 and CE 04 – **Scooter‑style comfort and practicality**
- **BMW CE 02:** Promoted as a **first‑time/urban beginner** bike with intuitive controls and low seat height.[3][9]
- **BMW CE 04:** Described as a **maxi‑scooter** with ~**80 miles real city range** and good comfort for daily use.[1]
- **Scooter advantages:** Step‑through design, good storage options, easy ergonomics, and very friendly for city traffic and frequent stops.
- **Use case:** You prefer scooter ergonomics, easy low‑speed handling, and practical storage for work bags or groceries.

## 4. How to decide what’s “best” for *your* daily city commute

Across multiple guides, a few **key factors** consistently define a good city EV motorcycle:[1][3][8]

- **Real city range:**  
  Aim for **50–100 miles real‑world city range** if you commute daily.[3]  
  Short commutes (<30 miles round trip) can use smaller packs; longer or unpredictable days benefit from 80–150+ miles.

- **Weight and agility:**  
  For urban use, bikes **under ~400–450 lb** feel much more manageable in stop‑and‑go riding and tight parking.[3][8]  
  Zero FXE, Can‑Am Pulse, and many scooters score well here.

- **Charging practicality:**  
  - **Removable batteries** help if you cannot install home charging or live in an apartment.[1][3]  
  - **J1772 / Level 2 compatibility** is important if you rely on public charging at work or in parking garages.[3][8]  
  - Fast charging (0–80% in ~40–60 minutes) is valuable if you do long days or ride beyond your usual commute.[3][6][8]

- **Use case match:**
  - **Under 30 miles/day, purely urban:** Budget models like **CSC City Slicker** or **Ryvid Anthem** are usually sufficient.[3][7][9]
  - **Up to ~60 miles/day, some faster roads but mostly city:** **Zero FXE**, **BMW CE 04**, or **Can‑Am Pulse** are strong choices.[1][3][8]
  - **Includes highway segments and you value time:** **LiveWire One / S2 Del Mar** or similar fast‑charging bikes.[3][6][8]
  - **Very long commutes or frequent trips beyond the city:** **Energica Experia / EsseEsse9+**, **Zero DSR/X**.[1][3][6][8][10]
