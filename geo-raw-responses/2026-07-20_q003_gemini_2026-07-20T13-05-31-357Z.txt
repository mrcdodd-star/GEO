--- RAW RESPONSE TEXT ---
For urban commuters, apartment dwellers, or riders without a garage, an electric motorbike with a **removable battery** is a game-changer. Instead of hunting down public chargers or dragging extension cords out of a window, you can simply slide the battery out, bring it indoors, and plug it into a standard wall outlet. 

Because battery weight is a major factor in how "easy" home charging actually is, the best electric motorbikes on the market use different strategies (such as split dual batteries or integrated wheels) to make transportation manageable.

---

### 1. **Maeving RM1S (Best Classic British Style)**
* **Category:** Retro Cafe Racer
* **Battery Setup:** Dual removable battery canisters (2.6 kWh each, total 5.2 kWh) housed in the lower frame. They slide out easily and can be placed on an optional desktop charging dock. 
* **Battery Weight:** ~33 lbs (15 kg) per battery. 
* **Top Speed:** 70 mph (112 km/h)
* **Range:** Up to 80 miles (128 km)
* **Estimated Price:** ~$10,000 / £7,495

**Why it’s top-rated:** 
Designed by former Triumph engineers, the Maeving RM1S looks like a stunning 1960s board-track racer but runs on a silent, modern hub motor. It has incredible build quality. Because the total battery capacity is split into two separate packs, carrying them inside is a highly manageable task compared to one massive block. It also supports on-board charging if you do happen to have access to a garage plug.

* **Pros:** Gorgeous vintage styling, split battery weight is easy on your back, premium components.
* **Cons:** Premium pricing; the desktop charging dock is usually sold as an extra accessory.

---

### 2. **Ryvid Anthem (Most Innovative Battery Transport)**
* **Category:** Modern Naked Commuter
* **Battery Setup:** Single 4.3 kWh lithium-ion battery pack. Ryvid solved the weight problem by building **fold-down wheels and a suitcase-style handle** directly into the battery housing. 
* **Battery Weight:** ~87 lbs (39 kg) with the on-board charger.
* **Top Speed:** 75+ mph (can hit up to 81 mph)
* **Range:** Up to 75 miles (120 km) in the city
* **Estimated Price:** ~$6,495

**Why it’s top-rated:** 
The Ryvid Anthem is incredibly popular for its value, futuristic design, and smooth ride. The battery is located in the lower frame to keep the center of gravity low. When you need to charge, a mechanical release drops the battery onto its wheels so you can roll it like a suitcase into your office or home. It also features an push-button adjustable-height seat (31" to 34"), which is perfect for riders of different heights.

* **Pros:** Highly stable ride, very affordable, no heavy lifting required to move the battery on flat ground.
* **Cons:** At 87 lbs, carrying the battery up stairs is extremely difficult without an elevator.

---

### 3. **Kawasaki Z e-1 & Ninja e-1 (Easiest to Carry)**
* **Category:** Lightweight Naked (Z) or Sport (Ninja) Bike
* **Battery Setup:** Dual removable battery packs (1.3 kWh each, 2.7 kWh total) wired in parallel. They sit under a lockable hatch where a gas tank would normally be. 
* **Battery Weight:** ~25 lbs (11.5 kg) per battery.
* **Top Speed:** 53 mph (up to 61 mph using a 15-second "e-boost" feature)
* **Range:** ~44 miles (70 km)
* **Estimated Price:** ~$7,300–$7,600 (often discounted at dealers)

**Why it’s top-rated:** 
If you want the peace of mind of a legacy motorcycle manufacturer, Kawasaki’s entry-level electrics are fantastic options. They are light, agile, and look exactly like their gas-powered Z and Ninja siblings. Because Kawasaki split the power source into two lightweight, 25-pound packs, these are by far the easiest batteries on this list to carry up stairs or transport. 

* **Pros:** Backed by Kawasaki's extensive dealer network/warranty; exceptionally lightweight batteries.
* **Cons:** Limited top speed and range compared to startups, making it strictly an urban/suburban commuter.

---

### 4. **Land Moto District (Best Dual-Sport / Scrambler)**
* **Category:** Street Tracker / Scrambler
* **Battery Setup:** Removable aluminum-cased "Core" battery packs. You can choose a single 2.1 kWh battery, dual 2.1 kWh batteries, or a larger 5.5 kWh monobloc. 
* **Battery Weight:** ~30 lbs (for the 2.1 kWh pack).
* **Top Speed:** Up to 70 mph (112 km/h)
* **Range:** Up to 120 miles (with the largest battery setup)
* **Estimated Price:** Starts at $6,995

**Why it’s top-rated:** 
Hand-built in Cleveland, Ohio, the Land Moto District is a minimal, raw, and incredibly fun machine. It has an upright, highly approachable riding position and is available in both "Street" and rugged "Scrambler" off-road configurations. The modular battery system allows you to buy a lightweight, lower-capacity option for cheap, or upgrade to a higher-capacity battery later if your commute gets longer.

* **Pros:** Highly customizable, incredibly lightweight chassis (~180 to 230 lbs total), great on and off road.
* **Cons:** Choosing the larger 5.5 kWh battery can push the price closer to $10,000.

---

### 5. **NIU RQi / RQi Sport (Best Tech & Tech-Savvy Features)**
* **Category:** Smart Naked Roadster
* **Battery Setup:** Dual 72V 36Ah lithium-ion batteries under the "tank".
* **Battery Weight:** ~50 lbs (22.5 kg) per battery.
* **Top Speed:** 68 mph (110 km/h)
* **Range:** ~65 miles (105 km)
* **Estimated Price:** ~$9,000 / €8,000

**Why it’s top-rated:** 
NIU is famous for its smart electric scooters, and the RQi is their first proper motorcycle. It comes loaded with premium tech: built-in 1080p front and rear dashcams, dual-channel ABS, Brembo brakes, traction control (rare on smaller electrics), and GPS anti-theft tracking through the NIU app. It feels robust, quiet, and highly futuristic.

* **Pros:** High-end safety features, robust build, highly connected app integration.
* **Cons:** The batteries are heavy and sit deep in the frame, making them physically demanding to lift out and carry.

---

### What to Keep in Mind Before Buying:
1. **Lifting Capacity vs. Living Situation:** If you live on the 3rd floor of a walk-up apartment, look at the **Kawasaki Z e-1** or **Maeving RM1S**. Lugging a single 50+ lb battery up stairs gets exhausting very quickly. If you have an elevator, the **Ryvid Anthem**’s wheeled battery is a perfect fit.
2. **License Requirements:** Depending on where you live, these bikes generally require a standard motorcycle license (or an A1/CBT learner's permit in the UK and Europe).
3. **Charger Location:** Most of these bikes allow you to plug directly into the motorcycle if you *do* have a garage, giving you the best of both worlds.
