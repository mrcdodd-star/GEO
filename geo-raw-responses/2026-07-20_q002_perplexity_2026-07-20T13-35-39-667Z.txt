Across recent 2025–2026 guides, stand‑out models for urban use include the **Zero FXE, LiveWire One / S2 Del Mar, BMW CE 02/CE 04, Can‑Am Pulse, Ryvid Anthem, CSC City Slicker, Super Soco TC Max**, and premium long‑range options like the **Energica Experia/EsseEsse9+**.[1][4][5][8]

Below is a practical breakdown focused specifically on *daily city commuting*.

---

### 1. Top all‑around city commuter: Zero FXE

Multiple independent guides name the **Zero FXE** as a top or “best value” urban EV motorcycle.[1][4][5][8]

- **Why it’s excellent for commuting**
  - About **100 miles real‑world city range**, enough for most daily round‑trips with margin.[1][4][8]
  - **Lightweight** (~386 lb) and narrow, ideal for filtering through traffic and tight parking.[4]
  - Uses Zero’s proven powertrain; strong torque and smooth throttle for stop‑and‑go riding.[4][5]
  - Modular battery system and J1772 compatibility simplify charging.[1][4]

If you want one bike that balances range, performance, and practicality in the city, the FXE is consistently recommended as a top choice.[4][5][8]

---

### 2. Fast, highway‑capable commuters: LiveWire One & LiveWire S2 Del Mar

If your “city commute” includes highway segments or you want strong performance:

- **LiveWire One**
  - Designed for urban and mixed commuting; offers **fast charging (0–80% in ~40 minutes)** via DC fast charge.[4][8]
  - City range reported around **140–150 miles** in recent buyer guides.[4][8]
  - Powerful acceleration and multiple ride modes for wet or variable conditions.[4][8]

- **LiveWire S2 Del Mar (and S2 Mohalland successor)**
  - Newer, more urban‑focused LiveWire platform; reviewers highlight **nimble handling and quick charging**.[1][6]
  - Around **120 miles urban range** with strong power, considered very practical for daily commuting.[1][6]

Choose one of these if you regularly use expressways, want premium features, and value fast charging over lowest purchase price.

---

### 3. Scooter‑style comfort and ease: BMW CE 02 and CE 04

For riders who prefer scooter ergonomics and twist‑and‑go simplicity:

- **BMW CE 04**
  - Ranked among top commuting EVs as a **maxi‑scooter** with about **80 miles real city range**.[1]
  - Upright, relaxed seating and good wind protection; designed specifically for urban mobility.[1]
- **BMW CE 02**
  - Highlighted as **ideal for first‑time riders** because of low seat height and intuitive controls.[4]
  - Compact size and simple operation make it well‑suited to dense downtown environments.[4]

Scooter‑like EVs are excellent if you prioritize ease of use, storage, and comfort over outright performance.

---

### 4. Nimble urban newcomer: Can‑Am Pulse

The **Can‑Am Pulse** is repeatedly called out as an urban‑focused machine:

- Around **99–100 miles city range** and agile handling targeted at dense traffic.[1][6][8]
- Mid‑price (~$13–14k), with performance and design optimized for city speeds.[1][6]

If you want something modern and city‑specific but not a scooter, the Pulse is a strong candidate.

---

### 5. Affordable short‑range commuters: CSC City Slicker, Ryvid Anthem, Super Soco TC Max

For daily commutes under ~30–40 miles round‑trip, lower‑cost bikes can be ideal.[4]

- **CSC City Slicker**
  - Often recommended as a **budget city EV** around or under **$5,000**.[4]
  - Real‑world urban range around **60 miles**, enough for shorter daily trips.[4]
- **Ryvid Anthem**
  - Mentioned as a good option for **weekday travel under ~60 miles** thanks to light weight and simple maintenance.[4]
- **Super Soco TC Max**
  - Cited as **“best for beginners”** in a multi‑bike comparison; inexpensive and forgiving, aimed at city riding.[5]
  - Top speed and range are sufficient for urban streets and short suburban links.[5][7]

Pick one of these if budget is key and your commute is relatively short, without long highway sections.

If your city commute includes rough roads, unpaved shortcuts, or you want something more playful:

- **Surron Ultra Bee**
  - Ranked among commuter EVs as a **dual‑sport** choice with **40–60 miles real city range** and a removable battery.[1]
- **E Ride Pro SS**
  - Recommended as a **sub‑$5K budget commuter** with decent speed and low maintenance, good for shorter urban trips.[4]

### 7. Premium long‑range commuters: Energica Experia and EsseEsse9+

If you want one bike for **city plus long trips** and cost is less of a concern:

- **Energica Experia**
  - Frequently cited as a **top long‑range electric motorcycle**, with up to **220 miles** of city range and full touring capability.[4][6][8]
  - Strong torque, comfort, and storage make it viable as a “commute + weekend touring” machine.[4][8]

These are more than most city commuters need, but they are ideal if you combine an urban ride with regular longer journeys.

### 8. How to choose the best commuter for *your* situation

Recent EV motorcycle commuting guides emphasize matching the bike to your **actual daily pattern**:[1][4][8]

- **Commute distance (round‑trip)**
  - Under **30 miles**: budget bikes like **CSC City Slicker, Super Soco TC Max, E Ride Pro SS** are sufficient.[4][5]
  - **30–60 miles**: mid‑range options like **Zero FXE, Ryvid Anthem, BMW CE 02/CE 04, Can‑Am Pulse**.[1][4][8]
  - Over **60 miles** or frequent highway: **LiveWire One / S2 Del Mar** or **Energica Experia**.[4][6][8]

- **Charging access**
  - Home only: prioritize **50–100+ mile city range** so you charge once a day or every few days.[1][4][8]
  - Public J1772/DC: bikes like **Zero FXE, LiveWire One, Energica** benefit from faster/top‑up charging.[4][8]

- **Experience level**
  - New riders: easier bikes/scooters such as **BMW CE 02, Super Soco TC Max, CSC City Slicker**.[4][5]
  - Experienced: performance‑oriented options like **LiveWire, Zero SR/F or DSRX, Energica** if your budget and needs justify them.[5][6][8]

- **Budget**
  - Under **$5k** (new or closeout): **CSC City Slicker, E Ride Pro SS, some Super Soco/HOVSCO commuter models**.[4][5][8]
  - **$6–12k**: **Zero FXE, BMW CE 04, Ryvid Anthem, Can‑Am Pulse (upper end)**.[1][4][6][8]
  - Above that: **LiveWire, Energica** and other premium brands.[4][5][6][8]
